Pest control businesses have specific software needs that generic invoicing or CRM tools don't cover: recurring service contracts, chemical tracking, route optimization, and customer portals where homeowners can view their service history. The right software handles all of this without requiring a full-time office manager.

Here's an honest comparison of the best pest control business software options available in 2026.

## What Pest Control Software Needs to Do

A pest control business isn't just scheduling one-time jobs — it's managing recurring customers across quarterly, monthly, and annual treatment cycles. The right platform handles:

- **Recurring service scheduling** — auto-schedule quarterly or monthly visits without manual rebooking
- **Route optimization** — group nearby jobs on the same day to minimize drive time
- **Chemical/product logging** — EPA compliance often requires logging what was applied, where, and at what concentration
- **Customer communication** — reminders before service, confirmations, and invoice delivery
- **Multi-technician dispatch** — if you run more than one van, the dispatch board matters
- **Mobile access for technicians** — service notes, completion sign-off, and photo documentation in the field

## Best Pest Control Business Software in 2026

### 1. FieldRoutes (by ServiceTitan) — Best for Mid-Sized Pest Control Companies

**Best for:** Pest control companies with 5–50 technicians who need purpose-built pest control software with recurring billing, route optimization, and compliance tracking.

FieldRoutes (formerly PestRoutes) is the category leader specifically for pest control. It handles recurring service plans natively, including subscription billing, automatic renewal scheduling, and service agreement management.

**Standout features:**
- Built-in route optimization (groups jobs by geography)
- Chemical application tracking for compliance
- Customer portal with service history
- Automated review requests after service

**Pricing:** Custom pricing; typically $200–$600+/month depending on company size.

**Limitation:** Expensive for small operations. Not ideal for a solo operator or 1–2 van business.

### 2. Jobber — Best Mid-Market Option for Pest Control

**Best for:** Pest control businesses with 2–10 technicians that want strong scheduling and customer communication without the enterprise price tag of FieldRoutes.

Jobber handles recurring service well, supports multi-technician dispatch, and has a client portal where customers can view upcoming appointments and service history. The quoting and invoicing are polished, and the customer notification system (automated appointment reminders by SMS/email) reduces no-shows.

**Limitation:** No chemical tracking or EPA compliance features. Not purpose-built for pest control — you'll work around its field service generics.

**Pricing:** $49–$249/month.

## How to Choose

**Solo or 1–2 vans, just getting started:** QuotArc or ServiceM8. Both are affordable, easy to set up, and handle the basics without locking you into an enterprise contract.

**2–8 vans, growing business:** Jobber. The best combination of features, usability, and price for this size. Add chemical tracking via a separate compliance log if required.

**8+ vans with route optimization needs:** FieldRoutes or WorkWave. The route optimization alone pays for itself at this scale.

**Strong compliance requirements (commercial pest control):** FieldRoutes. The chemical tracking and service record documentation are built for regulatory environments.

The common mistake: small pest control businesses buying FieldRoutes or WorkWave before they need the complexity. Start with something affordable, grow into the more powerful tools as your operation scales.
