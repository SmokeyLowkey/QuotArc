Managing field technicians without dispatch software is a logistics problem that gets worse as you grow. You're calling technicians to give them job details. You're calling customers to give them ETAs. You're manually tracking who's where. Dispatch software solves all of this — and the right option for a small service business is very different from what a 50-person operation needs.

Here's what to look for and which tools are actually worth it for small contractors.

## What Dispatch Software Does

Dispatch software gives you a live view of your field operations: which technicians are assigned to which jobs, where they are, whether jobs are running on time, and when they're done. For the technician, it delivers job details to their phone so they don't need a call from the office to know where to go next.

The core functions:

- **Job assignment** — create a job and assign it to a specific technician
- **Dispatch board** — visual calendar/map showing all active jobs by tech and time slot
- **Technician mobile app** — techs see their queue, update status, navigate to the job
- **Customer notifications** — automated SMS/email with ETA or completion notice
- **Real-time status updates** — office sees job progress as it happens

For a small operation (1–5 techs), you don't need GPS tracking of every movement or route optimization — you need clean job assignment, mobile access, and status updates.

## What Small Businesses Actually Need vs. What They Don't

**You need:**
- A clear visual of who's doing what today
- Techs getting job info on their phone without a call from you
- Customers getting notified when tech is on the way
- Status updates so you know when jobs complete

**You probably don't need (yet):**
- Real-time GPS tracking of every technician
- Route optimization across 10+ daily jobs
- Automated dispatch based on skill sets and territories
- Complex reporting dashboards

Most small service businesses overpay for dispatch complexity they never use. A $200/month enterprise dispatch platform is solving problems you don't have with 2–4 techs.

## What to Ask Before Choosing

**Does it work on mobile browsers?** Your technicians need to access job details from their phone. A native app is ideal, but a well-built mobile web interface works. Check whether techs can update job status and navigate to jobs without needing to download anything.

**How does job creation work?** If you have to manually enter job details that already exist in a quote, that's waste. The best tools convert approved quotes to jobs automatically.

**What do customers receive?** At minimum, automated notification when the tech is en route. Better tools include a link to track the tech in real time.

**Is there a per-user cost?** Many dispatch tools charge per technician. At 5 techs, a $10/user/month add-on becomes $50/month you weren't expecting.

## The Real Cost of Bad Dispatching

Poor dispatch coordination costs time on both ends:
- Techs call the office for job details instead of seeing them in an app
- Office staff make follow-up calls to confirm arrival
- Customers call asking for ETAs because no notification came
- Jobs run over because the next job wasn't visible to the tech

For a small operation running 5–10 jobs per day, even 15 minutes of wasted dispatch time per job adds up to 1–2 hours of lost productivity daily.
