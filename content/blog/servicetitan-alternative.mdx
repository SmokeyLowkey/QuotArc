ServiceTitan is one of the most powerful field service management platforms ever built. It's also designed for companies running 10+ technicians, charges upwards of $400/month (often much more at enterprise scale), and requires a formal onboarding process just to get started.

If you're a solo electrician, a 2–4 person electrical crew, or a small residential electrical contracting business, ServiceTitan is almost certainly the wrong tool. Here's why — and what the right alternatives are.

## Why Small Electricians Look for ServiceTitan Alternatives

**Cost.** ServiceTitan doesn't publish pricing publicly, which is a signal in itself. Reported costs typically start around $400–$500/month for small teams and scale steeply from there. For a solo operator doing $200,000–$400,000 per year in revenue, that's 1–3% of gross revenue going to software.

**Complexity.** ServiceTitan was designed for service companies with dedicated dispatch teams, marketing departments, and operations managers. The feature set is enormous. For a small electrical contractor, most of it is irrelevant, and the learning curve to get value from what you need is steep.

**Onboarding burden.** ServiceTitan typically requires a formal onboarding engagement before you can use the platform productively. For small operators, this means hours of setup time before you're even doing real work.

**Overkill for the job.** If you're quoting jobs, scheduling them, invoicing customers, and answering your phone — you need software that handles those four things well. You don't need demand generation analytics, technician performance scorecards, or enterprise call center integrations.

### 2. Jobber — Best Mid-Tier Option

**Best for:** Electrical contractors with 3–10 technicians who need stronger dispatching and CRM than QuotArc, without ServiceTitan's complexity.

Jobber is the most polished mid-market field service platform. It covers quoting, dispatching, invoicing, customer communication, and reporting better than most alternatives. The interface is genuinely well-designed, and the mobile app is reliable.

The downside is price — $129–$249/month depending on the features you need — and the absence of AI tools. Jobber doesn't have an AI phone receptionist or AI-assisted quoting.

**Pricing:** $49–$249/month.

### 3. Workiz — Best for Electricians Who Prioritize Dispatch

**Best for:** Electrical contractors where the primary bottleneck is scheduling multiple techs efficiently.

Workiz has a strong dispatching engine — drag-and-drop scheduling, real-time GPS for technicians, and automated customer notifications when a tech is on the way. If you're running a multi-tech shop and dispatch is your pain point, Workiz deserves a look.

**Pricing:** ~$45/month base, scales with user count.

## The Honest Recommendation

If you're a small electrical contractor looking for a ServiceTitan alternative, the decision usually comes down to one question: **are you a 1–5 truck operation or a 10+ truck operation?**
