Sending a quote in a Word document is leaving money on the table. It looks unprofessional, it's easy to lose, and you have no idea whether the customer even opened it. Modern contractor quoting software fixes all three problems — and the best options cost less than a tank of gas per month.

Here's an honest comparison of the best quoting tools for service contractors in 2026.

## What Good Contractor Quoting Software Actually Does

A quote is your first impression after the site visit. The right software makes that impression count:

- **Creates professional-looking quotes** with your logo, line items, and terms
- **Tracks opens** — you know exactly when the customer viewed the quote
- **Collects digital approval** — customer signs off online without printing anything
- **Converts to invoice with one click** — no re-entering data after the job is approved
- **Follows up automatically** — if the customer hasn't responded in 48 hours, sends a reminder

If your current quoting process doesn't do all five of these things, you're leaving efficiency (and probably revenue) on the table.

## What to Look for When Choosing

**Price list / rate book.** You shouldn't be typing in labour rates and material costs from scratch every time. Good quoting software lets you build a saved list and just select items.

**Customer-facing approval.** Email the quote, customer clicks approve, you get notified. No printing, no scanning, no phone calls just to confirm they want the job.

**Open tracking.** Knowing when a customer opened your quote tells you when to follow up. If they opened it 10 minutes ago and haven't responded, now is the time to call.

**Quote-to-invoice conversion.** Re-entering data from a quote into an invoice is wasted time. This should be one click.

**Mobile access.** You quote jobs in the field. Your quoting tool needs to work from your phone — whether that's a native app or a mobile-optimized browser experience.
